Project name:

Pokret za nepokretnu kulturnu baštinu

Details:

One of the biggest projects I worked on with a few university colleagues, now in its second year, needed a full brand built from scratch. My first sketch was quite minimal, but the team liked its composition and potential. Through a few iterations we focused on threading the project’s seven core colors in as accents. We kept much of last year’s layout and, in the end, didn’t replace the typefaces, although I explored several options. The final poster went through multiple rounds before print, and the lead organizer was very happy with the result. We also weighed the original concept side-by-side before committing to this direction.


We split tasks across the team, and I took on the main poster, a four-page brochure, and a suite of Instagram posts. Print had the tighter deadline, so I delivered that first and then finished the digital assets. In the brochure, the left spread presents the agenda, while the right spread is the exterior design. For social, I worked with the latest 2025 portrait formats and experimented with placing speakers on alternating sides; the current grid follows the color-wheel logic instead. Each hue has a role, every color represents its own specilization.
